What is a Close-Up?

A close-up is a commonly used shot in film photography, typically focusing on a character's face or other details (such as hands, objects, etc.) to highlight emotions, details, or emphasize specific information. By narrowing the shooting range, a close-up directs the audience's attention to particular emotional expressions or key details. Close-up shots of the eyes, in particular, are especially captivating to me, as they not only convey emotions most directly but also serve as a significant test of the actor's performance.
